## 2. Brand and identity system

## 3. One target customer

### Beachhead

> An owner of an operating US local service business who is preparing to request roughly $50,000–$500,000 in SBA-backed or conventional financing for expansion and needs to turn existing business knowledge and financial inputs into a professional plan.

Examples include cleaning, landscaping, construction trades, home care, childcare, wellness, and other local services. These are examples inside one segment, not separate campaigns.

### Qualification rules

A qualified prospect:

- operates a local service business in the United States;
- has a defined financing use, approximate amount, and intended lender or financing path;
- can provide pricing, service capacity, owner background, costs, and either operating history or explicit forecast assumptions;
- will complete the structured intake and respond to follow-up questions;
- understands that the service prepares a plan but does not guarantee approval; and
- does not require audited statements, legal advice, tax advice, a valuation, a custom financial workbook, or primary research.

### Deliberate exclusions during the first 10 customers

- venture-capital pitch plans for pre-revenue software startups;
- grant writing or grant discovery;
- immigration business plans;
- regulated securities offerings;
- buyers who want fabricated market facts or financial results;
- rush delivery before the intake and source materials are complete; and
- work requiring a CPA, attorney, appraiser, or licensed financial professional.

### Buyer problem and message

**Problem:** “I know my business, but I do not know how to organize the story, funding request, operations, and financial assumptions into a document a lender can review.”

**Core message:**

> Turn what you know about your business into a professional, funding-focused plan—through a structured intake and direct human review.

**Support points:**

1. The structured intake exposes missing inputs before drafting begins.
2. The plan connects the funding request to operations, customer acquisition, and financial assumptions.
3. The buyer receives editable DOCX and shareable PDF files.
4. Ben reviews every deliverable and manages the two revision rounds.

## 4. One final offer

### Funding-Focused Business Plan Service — $750

**Outcome:** a coherent business plan organized for a small-business financing conversation and tailored to the buyer's stated lender, purpose, and business.

**Included:**

- structured written intake and one round of follow-up questions;
- 20-minute kickoff or clarification call when needed;
- executive summary;
- company, ownership, and funding-purpose sections;
- services, pricing, target customer, and competitive-position analysis;
- sales, marketing, operations, management, milestones, and risk sections;
- funding request and use-of-funds narrative;
- three-year projection summary and financial narrative based on buyer-provided facts and assumptions;
- explicit labeling of material assumptions and information gaps;
- human editorial and consistency review;
- DOCX and PDF delivery; and
- two consolidated revision rounds.

**Not included:**

- independent or primary market research;
- a promise that every market statement has an external citation;
- lender approval or a claim that any lender has endorsed the plan;
- an editable spreadsheet model, pitch deck, grant application, valuation, or legal/accounting advice;
- creation of missing historical financial records; or
- a new business concept, audience, or funding scenario during revisions.

**Delivery promise:** seven calendar days beginning when Ben accepts the intake as complete. If required information is missing, Ben asks for it before accepting the delivery clock or requests a documented extension through the marketplace.

**Revision policy:** two revision rounds are included. A round is one consolidated list of changes submitted within seven calendar days of delivery. Revisions refine the agreed plan using the original business, financing purpose, and supplied data. A new concept, financing audience, material data set, or custom analysis is a new scope.

**Cancellation and refund policy:** confirm fit before accepting the work. If Ben identifies a scope mismatch before accepting the intake, cancel and refund the order. If Business Plan Writer cannot deliver the agreed scope, provide a full refund. After the first complete draft is delivered, a change of mind, change in business direction, or financing denial is not refundable; use the included revisions for in-scope corrections. Fiverr orders also remain subject to Fiverr's controlling cancellation and dispute terms. State the equivalent direct-order terms at checkout and in the contract.

**Pricing rule:** use $750 everywhere during the validation window. Do not show an artificial crossed-out price. Reassess only after five completed customers:

- raise to $950 if at least 20% of qualified conversations convert, delivery is on time, and median hands-on time is at or below six hours;
- hold at $750 if quality is strong but conversion or delivery data is inconclusive;
- change scope or target before discounting if qualified conversion is below 10%; and
- do not add lower and higher tiers until recurring buyer objections show a real packaging need.

## 5. Marketplace and channel sequence

### Now: one offer, three demand paths

1. **Fiverr:** passive high-intent storefront and review engine.
2. **Ben's LinkedIn profile and Service Page:** trust, education, and inbound messages.
3. **Manual direct outreach:** warm network, financing-adjacent referral partners, and small-business owners with a visible financing or expansion trigger.

These are not three brands or three offers. Every path points to the same service, price, sample, qualification rules, and intake.

### Next marketplace: Upwork

Add Upwork after five completed engagements or day 31, whichever is later. Upwork is the next test because buyers post explicit projects and proposals can include relevant work samples. It also charges freelancers a contract-specific fee currently ranging from 0% to 15%, and most proposals consume Connects, so it should be a selective job-response channel rather than another profile to maintain casually: [Upwork service fees](https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/211062538-Learn-about-the-Freelancer-Service-Fee), [submitting proposals](https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/211062998-How-to-submit-a-proposal-on-Upwork), and [Connects](https://support.upwork.com/hc/en-us/articles/211062898-Understanding-and-using-Connects).

Upwork operating rule:

- apply to no more than three strong-fit jobs per week for the first four weeks;
- use only jobs matching the target, scope, budget, and timeline;
- attach the fictional sample and a one-page process overview;
- do not boost proposals until the unboosted interview rate is known; and
- stop after 12 qualified proposals if none produce an interview, then revise profile/proposal before spending more Connects.

Do not add Contra, a separate LinkedIn company content page, Instagram, TikTok, or automated social publishing during this validation window. The website remains a credibility and conversion surface, not a traffic strategy.

**Is loan approval guaranteed?**

No. The service prepares and organizes the plan for review. The lender controls its requirements and decision.

**Do you perform market research?**

Independent or primary research is not included. The plan analyzes the market and competition using your source materials, known competitors, and clearly labeled assumptions. Ask for a custom quote if your lender requires a separately sourced study.

**Are the financials accountant-prepared?**

No. The projection summary and narrative are built from the facts and assumptions you provide and are not a CPA opinion, audit, or guarantee.

**Can you follow my lender's format?**

Usually, if you send the requirements before ordering. I will confirm fit first.

**What counts as a revision?**

One consolidated list of refinements to the agreed business, funding purpose, and data. A new concept, audience, or material data set is new scope.

**Do you use AI?**

I use software-assisted drafting and quality checks inside my workflow. I personally review, edit, and deliver every plan and remain accountable for the service.

## 9. Delivery workflow for the first 10 customers

1. **Qualify before purchase:** financing purpose, amount, lender, deadline, business stage, source data, excluded needs.
2. **Accept intake:** confirm in writing that required inputs are complete and state the delivery date.
3. **Run intake gap review:** distinguish customer facts, customer estimates, unknowns, and material assumptions.
4. **Draft with software support:** generate internal sections and checks; never deliver raw output.
5. **Human QA:** verify names, numbers, arithmetic, internal consistency, scope, unsupported assertions, formatting, and privacy.
6. **Deliver:** DOCX, PDF, concise assumption/gap note, and instructions for one consolidated revision list.
7. **Revise:** preserve original facts and agreed audience; record why each meaningful change was made.
8. **Close:** private score, review ask if earned, separate proof permission, referral ask, and dashboard entry.

For the first three customers, complete a second read after a break or on the next morning before delivery. Do not use a real-client document as a public sample without explicit written permission covering the exact excerpt and attribution.

## 10. Ad-readiness checklist

Paid ads are justified only when every **required** gate below is true. The calendar date alone does not unlock ads.

### Required gates

- [ ] At least five paid engagements have been completed; at least three match the chosen target.
- [ ] At least three customers have provided public reviews or explicit testimonial permission.
- [ ] At least 70% of completed customers say the plan is useful enough for their next financing conversation.
- [ ] Median usefulness score is at least 8/10.
- [ ] At least 90% of projects are delivered on or before the accepted delivery date.
- [ ] Refund/cancellation rate is below 10%.
- [ ] Median hands-on labor is known and at or below six hours, or the price has been raised to support the actual labor.
- [ ] Model/tool cost, payment/marketplace fees, and owner labor are recorded for every order.
- [ ] The direct offer produces positive contribution after valuing Ben's time at the chosen internal hourly rate.
- [ ] One landing page presents the same target, scope, $750 price, delivery, revisions, sample, boundaries, and CTA.
- [ ] Analytics capture source → qualified inquiry → checkout → intake complete → delivered → refunded.
- [ ] At least 10 qualified organic conversations have occurred, with a qualified-conversation-to-paid rate of 20% or higher.
- [ ] Delivery capacity exists for the orders a test might create.
- [ ] The exact legal seller, terms, privacy language, cancellation policy, and checkout support path are live.

### First paid experiment

When the gates pass, start with **high-intent search**, not broad social prospecting. Use one landing page, one target, one offer, and a small capped learning budget. A reasonable first test is $300–$500 or five target acquisition costs, whichever is lower.

Set the test CPA ceiling from economics, not hope:

> Maximum test CPA = no more than 30% of contribution before advertising

Where:

> Contribution before advertising = collected revenue − refunds − payment/marketplace fees − model/tool cost − contractor cost − owner labor valued at the internal hourly rate

Example only: if direct contribution before ads is $300, the initial CPA ceiling is $90. Do not use Fiverr order economics to justify direct-site ad spend, and do not scale on clicks or leads without paid completions and quality data.

Stop or revise the ad test when:

- the capped budget is spent without a qualified conversation;
- five qualified paid clicks/conversations show the same trust or scope objection;
- acquired customers have materially worse fit, refund, revision, or satisfaction results than organic customers; or
- fulfillment threatens the seven-day promise.

### 30-day decision rules

**Continue and deepen the niche** when there are at least five paid completions, at least 70% target fit, median usefulness of 8+, positive direct contribution, and repeatable objections/process issues.

**Iterate one variable** when qualified conversations occur but fewer than 20% buy. Choose one based on evidence:

- trust problem → improve fictional sample, process visibility, or permitted proof;
- scope problem → clarify included/excluded work;
- target problem → narrow the trigger or owner stage;
- price problem → first verify that buyers understand the scope and alternatives; do not automatically discount.

**Stop and reconsider the offer** when fewer than five qualified conversations occur after 100 genuinely targeted contacts and consistent follow-up. This is not evidence that no demand exists; it is evidence that the selected audience/channel/message combination did not produce enough signal.

## 12. Risks and assumptions

| Risk / assumption | Consequence | Mitigation or test |
|---|---|---|
| Business Plan Writer is descriptive and may be difficult to protect as a distinctive mark. | Naming conflicts, weak trademark protection, or domain constraints. | Run legal/name/domain checks before investing in identity assets. Validation can proceed with the descriptive service name. |
| The exact legal relationship between Ben, Sproutflow, and the payment account is unknown. | Inconsistent contracts, invoices, tax records, or card statements. | Confirm the legal seller before direct checkout. Keep the public service name consistent while disclosing the exact payee. |
| $750 willingness to pay has not been validated with this narrow target. | Low conversion or the wrong buyers. | Hold one price for the first 10 qualified conversations; diagnose trust, scope, urgency, and fit before changing price. |
| Fiverr has no review history for this offer. | Slow impressions and conversion. | Use a strong fictional sample, fast qualified responses, precise scope, and direct/LinkedIn demand in parallel. Never manufacture reviews. |
| The current product does not perform reliably sourced research. | Unsupported facts could damage credibility. | Do not market “real research.” Base work on client materials and labeled assumptions; manually source and cite only when separately scoped and actually completed. |
| “SBA” and “lender-focused” may be read as approval expertise. | Expectation or liability risk. | State that lender requirements vary, confirm the checklist before order, and never guarantee approval or imply lender endorsement. |
| Weak intake creates generic output. | Rework, missed deadlines, and dissatisfaction. | Do not accept the delivery clock until critical fields are complete; label unknowns and assumptions. |
| Seven-day delivery may be too aggressive while the workflow is immature. | Late delivery and poor reviews. | Cap capacity at two active projects per week and track hands-on hours. Extend before accepting incomplete work. |
| Fiverr fees compress margin. | A profitable direct offer may be unprofitable on-platform. | Calculate contribution by channel. Treat Fiverr as validation/review acquisition only while its economics remain acceptable. |
| One niche may initially produce too little volume. | Temptation to broaden prematurely. | Require 100 targeted contacts and 10 qualified conversations before judging the niche. Broaden one boundary at a time. |
| Public examples may expose client details or unapproved claims. | Privacy, trust, and legal risk. | Use only the fictional Bywater Grounds sample until explicit written permission exists. |
| Content activity can become a substitute for selling. | High engagement with no demand evidence. | Cap publishing at three LinkedIn posts per week and prioritize qualified conversations and paid completions. |
